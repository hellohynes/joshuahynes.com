Millions of developers use Stack Overflow every day, consisting of a knowledge base of over 10,000,000 questions. Yet not every questions gets asked. Privacy concerns, propriety information, or a niche audience keeps these questions locked within email chains, chat messages, or abandoned wikis.

Stack Overflow set out to change that by taking the tool developers love, and privatize it.

Problem

Starting from zero

Starting a community presents unique problems. Your efforts are focused on generating some sort of momentum and hopefully establish new, lasting norms within a group of people. Once a community has started, the problems morph toward building on your momentum to solidify the community. But the community at that point is started. One of the biggest hurdles of community building has been overcome: starting one.
